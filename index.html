He is a Humboldt research fellow carrying out his project, entitled "Video analysis and processing for multiple distributed wearable devices" in cooperation with his host, Prof. Dr.-Ing. Andre Kaup, Chair of Multimedia Communications and Signal Processing, University of Erlangen-Nuremberg, Germany from 2015. Before that, he was a postdoctoral researcher with Biomedical Research Imaging Center, University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, USA in 2014. He received the Ph.D. degree with the Coding and Visual Communication Group, Technical University of Denmark, Denmark in 2013, where he worked on the distributed video coding and processing project (2010~2013) with his supervisor, Prof. Soren Forchhammer. He received the M.Sc. degree in computer engineering and information technology from University of Ulsan, Korea, in 2009, working on medical image processing on massively parallel processor arrays with his supervisor, Prof. Jong Myon Kim, in the Embedded System Laboratory (2007~2009).
